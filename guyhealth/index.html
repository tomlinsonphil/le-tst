Heart — Three Narrowed Arteries Decision Pending
Left main artery narrowed 70-90%, plus two further narrowings (LAD and RCA). All still flowing at rest (TIMI 3). Repeat angiogram 29 July. Stent vs bypass surgery not yet decided.
Explain this to me
The heart is fed by three main pipes. All three have significant narrowings. The most critical is the "left main" — the trunk from which two branches grow. 70-90% narrowed means a very tight squeeze. Blood is still getting through at rest (that's what TIMI 3 means, on a scale of 0–3), which is why Dad hasn't had a heart attack. But it needs treating. The debate is between inserting a tiny metal tube to prop the artery open (stent), or bypass surgery where a healthy vessel is rerouted around all three narrowings. Philip and Dad's own cardiologist Dr Unal both favour bypass surgery for this pattern of disease.
Knee Infection (S. aureus) Resolving
MSSA Staphylococcus aureus — the treatable type. Two surgical washouts completed (26 Jun, 4 Jul). On oral clindamycin until 14 Aug. CRP falling. Sutures out 24 Jul.
Explain this to me
S. aureus is a common skin bacterium that entered Dad's bloodstream (probably from his psoriatic scalp) and settled in the knee joint. It caused a serious joint infection requiring two operations to wash it out. The first washout on 26 June was keyhole; the second on 4 July was open surgery as the infection had spread to the tissues around the knee. Antibiotics are needed until mid-August to clear it completely. CRP is a blood marker of infection — it's been falling, which means the infection is resolving.
DVT — Calf Blood Clot Being Treated
Small clot in left fibular vein (calf), found 10 July. Treated with apixaban (blood thinners) for 3 months and class 3 compression stocking daily. Repeat scan 10 October.
Explain this to me
A DVT (deep vein thrombosis) is a blood clot in a deep vein — in Dad's case a small one in the calf, the least dangerous location. Blood thinners prevent it growing or travelling to the lung. The compression stocking helps blood flow back up the leg. The clot almost certainly formed because he was immobile in hospital for weeks. It will be rescanned in October to confirm it has dissolved.
Scalp Psoriasis Needs Treatment
Almost certainly psoriasis, not eczema. Probable source of the original S. aureus infection. Coal tar shampoo + Betadine Scrub regime started. Dermatology appointment 27 July.
Explain this to me
Psoriatic skin is colonised by S. aureus bacteria in up to 80% of cases. The most likely explanation for how Dad's knee got infected is that bacteria from his scalp entered the bloodstream during scratching and settled in the knee — a well-documented pathway. While the scalp remains active and untreated there is ongoing risk of reinfection. The dermatologist on 27 July needs to know about the recent knee infection and any planned cardiac surgery, as it changes what treatments they can offer.

Why is he on so many blood-thinning medications?
Dad is on two types of blood-thinning medication at once: clopidogrel (prevents clots in arteries — heart protection) and apixaban (prevents the calf clot from growing). This is necessary but increases bleeding risk, which is one important reason why the choice between stent and bypass matters. A stent would require adding a third blood-thinning tablet for 6–12 months, whereas bypass surgery only needs aspirin afterwards — much simpler and safer alongside the apixaban.

Cardiovascular optimisation
Current diet is a good baseline Low processed food, lean protein, vegetables. The tweaks below are targeted refinements — not a wholesale change.

— Add: lentils or beans 2x/week Soluble fibre, plant protein, LDL lowering. Lentil soup is easy and cheap.
How much difference does diet actually make?
Dietary modification for established coronary artery disease is worthwhile but the effect size is modest compared to medications. The atorvastatin alone will do more for Dad's cholesterol than any dietary change. The Mediterranean diet evidence (PREDIMED trial) shows roughly 30% reduction in cardiovascular events — real, but his situation requires surgical intervention beyond diet alone. Think of dietary tuning as protecting the investment of whatever cardiac surgery achieves, not as a substitute for it. The porridge swap and the oily fish are the two highest-yield changes.
